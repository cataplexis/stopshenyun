The Issue

What is Shen Yun?

Shen Yun is a nationally touring Chinese dance show. While it advertises itself as a performing arts venture, it is actually operated by Falun Gong, a religious cult. These "dance" shows actually include cult propaganda content and are used as a way for Falun Gong to recruit as well as generate revenue.

Falun Gong: A Dangerous Cult

Falun Gong presents itself as a harmless meditation movement, but in reality, its leader, Li Hongzhi, seeks complete control over his followers.

They are urged to cut off contact with family members who don’t believe in Falun Gong, creating an isolating environment so they are easier to take advantage of. Falun Gong leaves no room for their disagreement. Followers are taught that criticizing the movement corrupts their spirit. Those who try to leave are gaslighted, shamed, and threatened with eternal punishment.

These vulnerable followers are taught that illness, misfortune, and even natural disasters are punishments for impure thoughts or behaviors. Followers are discouraged from questioning the teachings of Li Hongzhi, who claims to have supernatural powers and says he can “purify” people who are loyal enough.This creates a culture of fear, where doubt is seen as a threat to one’s spiritual survival.

In addition, Falun Gong’s teachings explicitly discourage medical treatment. Instead, they are told that spiritual “cultivation” will cure disease and prolong life. As a result, some followers have died from treatable illnesses, including children and elderly people who were pressured to avoid hospitals.

Falun Gong: Discriminatory Teachings

Falun Gong promotes discriminatory teachings that cause harm to nonmembers too. These views include calling LGBTQ+ people “degenerate” or “unclean”, the racist belief that mixed-race children are "unnatural," and the ableist belief that people with disabilities or mental illness are “paying for past-life sins.” Falun Gong actively stigmatizes and dehumanizes these communities.

Shen Yun and Falun Gong

Shen Yun doesn’t just fund performances, it helps fuel a misinformation empire. The money earned from Shen Yun shows goes to Falun Gong, which uses it to fund The Epoch Times, a far-right media outlet known for promoting conspiracy theories, QAnon content, and COVID-19 misinformation as well as the cult's discriminatory beliefs listed above. The flow of money doesn't stop there. In 2024, Epoch Times CFO Bill Guan was found to committed money laundering to the tune of $67 million, all of which he transfered into Epoch Times accounts.

Financial Fraud

Yet Falun Gong continues to be greedy for money. Its leader, Li Hongzhi, lives a life of luxury while coercing his followers to give up all of their money for the cause. Meanwhile, Shen Yun dancers and musicians are paid next to nothing despite all of the profit the show brings in.

In 2014, Falun Gong member Mr. Liang left the cult after realizing its predatory environment. However, his mother decided to stay, and Li Hongzhi convinced her to sell the house she had owned in Maryland since the 1980s for hundreds of thousands of dollars. As time went on, her health deteriorated, but Falun Gong convinced her not to see a doctor. By 2019, when Mr. Liang's mother finally caved after pleading from her children, she found that she had late stage liver cancer. The price of Falun Gong for her? low chances of survival and no money for treatment either, with hundreds of thousands in funds already given away to the cult.

Eight former Shen Yun performers revealed that they were not paid at all in their first year of labor, despite working fifteen hour days. Even after years of service, performers in their mid-20s still only report salaries of $12,000 per year.

Buy tickets to Shen Yun does not support the performers. It only gives more money to the greedy, dangerous cult Falun Gong, who will use it to spread misinformation and harmful views.

Shen Yun: Exploitation of Performers

Shen Yun dancers and musicians have to endure restrictive rules, abuse, and invasive control over their personal lives. Dancers report being publically shamed for their weight. They are also prohibited from using social media or contacting outsiders. Dancers as young as 12 are sent to a training compound in New York, barred from leaving, and not allowed any contact with their parents. Former members say they were punished for minor offenses and subjected to religious indoctrination for hours on end. Due to Falun Gong not believing in medical treatment, peformers are forced to ignore excruciating injuries like dislocated kneecaps and sprained ankles and perform anyways.

Cheng Qingling, a former dancer, had an untreated injury that made her left arm go numb. Kate Huang, former dancer, dislocated her kneecap before a performance in Seattle, only for her supervisor to give her a bag of ice and force her to perform for two hours anyways. Falun Gong never offered her treatment, and to this day, her knee never regained the same range of motion. Daisy Wang, former dancer who started touring with Shen Yun when she was 13, sprained her ankle five times in eight years but never asked for a doctor because she was told that medicine would make her spiritually corrupt. Twelve such former Shen Yun dancers report similar untreated injuries.

Shen Yun musicians face similar treatment. David Fiedler, former Shen Yun musician, experienced "tremendous psychological pressure to conform" to Falun Gong dogma. Mr. An, former musician, had to stitch up his own cuts with a hotel sewing kit because nobody offered to take him to a doctor. Joshua Lin, former Shen Yun violinist, told his classmates he felt pain in his shoulder. They told him it was a sign of his spiritual weakness. The reality? a bulging disc in his spine.

If they try to leave, they are threatened with eternal damnation. Shen Yun's treatment of performers is clearly abusive and should not be allowed to go on.

Shen Yun: Legal Controversies

Multiple lawsuits against Shen Yun and Falun Gong for labor trafficking, coercion, and psychological abuse are ongoing.

Chang v. Shen Yun Performing Arts Inc, filed by former Shen Yun dancer Chang Chun Ko, accuses Shen Yun of forced labor, human trafficking, visa fraud, and New York labor law violations. Chang started dancing with Shen Yun at 11 years old.

Sun Zan & Cheng Qingling v. Shen Yun, filed by former dancers Sun Zan and Cheng Qingling, is a separate but similar suit accusing Shen Yun of forced child labor, abusive training conditions, and psychological coercion. The complaint details physical injuries, fears of punishment, prolonged emotional trauma from isolation, and low wages.

New York State Department of Labor Investigation, started by the New York department of labor after revelations of labor abuse and child exploitation, is an investigation into Shen Yun’s labor practices and alleged longstanding violations of child labor laws.

Federal Visa Fraud Investigation, carried out by the State Department’s Diplomatic Security Service and Homeland Security Investigations, is a criminal investigation currently being carried out against Shen Yun
